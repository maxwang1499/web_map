NYC Building Emission Map

Check your building's emission and LL97 Compliance status.

70% of NYC's greenhouse gas emission comes from the buildings.
In order to reach its 2050 carbon neutrality goal, NYC passed Local Law 97, which places building-specific carbon thresholds on any building with gross square footage over 25,000 square feet and changes a penalty for every unit of carbon that exceeds the threshold. The law kicks in 2024, with a more stringent threshold planned out in 2030.
Hover over a circle to see information about the building's carbon emission and penalty.
Color of markers denotes penalty status. Green: no penalty; Orange: subject to penalty starting 2030; Red: Subject to penalty starting in 2024.
